# qjz042 — PAPER_TAKEAWAYS

## Main argument

The paper argues that racial income disparities in the United States persist across generations because black Americans, especially black men, experience much lower upward mobility and higher downward mobility than whites even conditional on parent income.

## Core mechanism

The black-white intergenerational gap is not mainly explained by parent income, education, or marital status. It is concentrated among men, persists within the same neighborhoods, and narrows only in a small set of low-poverty neighborhoods with low racial bias and high black father presence.

## Key numbers

- Black men earn about 10 percentile ranks less than white men conditional on parent income.
- Black women earn about 1 percentile rank more than white women conditional on parent income.
- White boys have higher adult incomes than black boys in 98.7% of Census tracts conditional on parent income.
- Fewer than 5% of black children grow up in the kinds of neighborhoods where gaps are relatively small.

## Candidate highlights

- The black-white gap is really a black-male mobility gap.
- Family background controls do little to close the gap.
- The gap survives same-neighborhood comparisons.
- Good neighborhoods for black boys are rare and have a specific social profile.
- Hispanic-white gaps shrink across generations, unlike black-white gaps.

## Figure Budget Selection

Required in this figure:
- One dominant contrast showing that the conditional income gap is large for black men and absent for black women.
- One support layer showing that the gap persists across place and that small-gap places are rare.

Deferred to later versions or notes:
- Hispanic and Asian intergenerational trajectories.
- Detailed tract-level correlates beyond the three neighborhood cues.
- The within-tract reduction from 10.0 to 7.7 percentile ranks.

Excluded from this figure and why:
- Full race-by-parent-rank mobility curves, because they widen scope and weaken the gender-specific claim.
- Maps, because they communicate dispersion but not the sharper substantive contrast.
- Family-structure and wealth controls, because they are important for identification but not necessary for this quick-read figure.

## Selected highlights for figure

- Main claim: the black-white mobility gap is concentrated among men.
- Support 1: the gap persists in almost every neighborhood.
- Support 2: the neighborhoods where the gap narrows are rare and socially distinctive.

## Deferred highlights

- American Indian persistence and Hispanic convergence.
- Employment and incarceration mechanisms by gender.
- Movers evidence showing better outcomes for boys who move earlier into better neighborhoods.
